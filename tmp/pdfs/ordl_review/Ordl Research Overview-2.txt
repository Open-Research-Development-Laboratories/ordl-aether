ORDL Research Overview
Open Research and Development Laboratories
Institutional Overview
Open Research and Development Laboratories (ORDL) is an independent nonprofit research organization
established to support open scientific inquiry, interdisciplinary development, and collaborative
technological advancement. Formed in 2021 as a domestic nonprofit public corporation in West
Virginia, ORDL was created to provide a formal research structure through which individuals can explore,
develop, and publish work across a broad range of technical and scientific domains.
ORDL was founded on the belief that meaningful innovation emerges when research remains open, shared,
and accessible rather than siloed behind institutional or commercial barriers. Its incorporation materials
define the organization’s purpose as providing “an open, broad and undefined spectrum of research and
development” in service of advancing society, while building systems that bridge multiple modalities and
technologies to create a more unified and collaborative world.
This framing is important. ORDL is not narrowly positioned as a single-product company or a conventional
software venture. It is structured as a laboratory: a research environment intended to host
experimentation, systems thinking, open development, and technically ambitious work guided by long-term
public benefit.
Mission and Philosophy
ORDL stands for a model of research in which technical progress and openness are not in tension with one
another , but mutually reinforcing. The organization’s governing documents emphasize several recurring
principles:
open research and development as a public good
freedom for individuals to educate themselves and invent
interdisciplinary collaboration across multiple fields and modalities
public release of work with appropriate contributor credit
defensive protection of intellectual output where necessary, not to restrict access, but to prevent
restriction by others
Taken together , these principles define ORDL as a laboratory committed to preserving the conditions under
which discovery can remain collaborative. Its philosophy is that innovation should not depend solely on
closed institutional pathways, but should also be cultivated in open ecosystems where researchers,
engineers, and builders can contribute meaningfully to shared progress.

Organizational Character
Legally, ORDL is organized as a nonprofit public corporation with the trade name ORDL. It was filed in West
Virginia in early August 2021 and operates with a nonprofit structure intended to support
charitable, educational, and scientific purposes consistent with 501(c)(3) standards. The
organization’s records also reflect an early federal exemption workflow and an IRS e-postcard filing tied to
EIN 87-1952983.
This structure matters because it places ORDL in a position distinct from many informal research collectives.
It is not merely a loose network of projects; it is a formal entity capable of serving as a platform for grants,
partnerships, technical publication, and collaborative development.
Research Identity
ORDL’s core identity is best understood as a platform for research-driven development. It exists to support
people who have a genuine desire to research and build in their chosen domains, while contributing to a
broader organizational vision of societal advancement through open and collaborative work.
That model lends itself especially well to frontier technical fields where architecture, experimentation, and
systems integration matter as much as finished products. In practical terms, ORDL is well positioned to
support work in areas such as:
artificial intelligence and intelligent systems
computational architectures and automation
open technical infrastructure
safety-conscious system design
interdisciplinary technology development
experimental tools, prototypes, and public research artifacts
Even where project areas vary, the unifying principle remains consistent: ORDL is designed to be an
enabling structure for serious technical work that is exploratory, mission-driven, and publicly meaningful.
Open Science and Knowledge Access
One of the strongest differentiators in ORDL’s institutional posture is its explicit commitment to openness.
Its founding materials do not describe knowledge as something to be hoarded or tightly enclosed. Instead,
they repeatedly frame research as something that should be carried out freely, credited properly, and
released openly to the public.
This is a powerful foundation for building credibility in research settings. It aligns naturally with academic
collaboration, public-interest technology, reproducible experimentation, and open publication practices. It
also creates a coherent ethical basis for future partnerships with institutions that care about transparency,
accountability, and public benefit.

Intellectual Property Position
ORDL’s documents include an unusually clear position on patents: intellectual property may be placed
under the organization not to hide information from the public, but to protect it from being withheld by
other parties. This is best understood as a defensive IP philosophy.
That stance gives ORDL a balanced and credible position. It avoids the naivete of assuming all research
environments are purely open by default, while also rejecting the idea that value must come from secrecy.
In institutional terms, this makes ORDL legible as an organization that understands both the importance of
openness and the practical need to safeguard research from enclosure or misuse.
Why ORDL Matters
The broader significance of ORDL lies in the kind of research institution it is trying to become. Many
organizations either overemphasize branding without substantive technical identity or produce technically
serious work without a coherent institutional narrative. ORDL has the foundation for something more
compelling: a laboratory model that combines legal legitimacy, open research principles, and a broad
technical mandate.
That combination gives it the potential to mature into a credible independent research organization capable
of producing software systems, technical papers, safety research, computational tools, and interdisciplinary
collaborations. With stronger public presentation, clearer publication pathways, and more polished
documentation, ORDL can be positioned not simply as a nonprofit with projects, but as a serious research
entity with a distinctive philosophy of open technological development.
Strategic Positioning for Grants and Partnerships
For grants, fellowships, and research collaborations, ORDL should be presented as:
An independent nonprofit research laboratory focused on open, interdisciplinary, safety-conscious
technological development.
That framing is strong because it is both accurate and expandable. It is grounded in the incorporation
documents, but it also leaves room for future specialization in AI systems, model evaluation, interpretability,
robustness, safety engineering, computational science, and other technical programs.
Institutions evaluating ORDL should come away with four impressions:
it is real and properly organized
it has a principled research philosophy
it supports technically serious work
it is trying to build public-value research infrastructure rather than a closed commercial silo

Short Institutional Biography
Open Research and Development Laboratories (ORDL) is an independent nonprofit research organization
dedicated to advancing open scientific inquiry, interdisciplinary development, and collaborative
technological innovation. Established in 2021 as a public nonprofit corporation, ORDL was
founded to provide a formal research structure for the exploration and development of emerging
technologies, computational systems, and public-interest technical work.
ORDL operates on the principle that meaningful progress is best achieved through open and collaborative
research ecosystems. Its mission emphasizes broad, cross-disciplinary experimentation, public access to
knowledge, contributor credit, and responsible stewardship of intellectual work. The organization supports
a model in which researchers and builders are empowered to pursue ambitious technical work while
contributing to a wider vision of societal advancement through shared innovation.
By combining nonprofit legitimacy with an explicitly open research philosophy, ORDL represents an effort to
build research infrastructure that is transparent, mission-driven, and oriented toward long-term public
benefit.
Founder Biography
Aaron Ferguson is the founder and principal organizer behind Open Research and Development
Laboratories (ORDL), an independent nonprofit research organization established to support open,
interdisciplinary technological development. His work is centered on building research-oriented technical
systems and institutional structures that enable exploration across multiple domains while preserving
openness, contributor credit, and public accessibility of knowledge.
Through ORDL, Ferguson has worked to create a formal laboratory framework for collaborative research
and development, grounded in the belief that innovation should remain both technically ambitious and
publicly meaningful. His approach reflects a combination of systems thinking, experimental development,
and mission-driven organizational design, with an emphasis on creating durable infrastructure for future
research initiatives.
Recommended Public Positioning
For websites, applications, and outreach, the clearest one-sentence description is:
Open Research and Development Laboratories (ORDL) is an independent nonprofit research
laboratory advancing open, interdisciplinary work in intelligent systems, computational
infrastructure, and public-interest technology.
And for shorter bios:
ORDL is a nonprofit research laboratory dedicated to open scientific inquiry, collaborative
development, and responsible technological innovation.
